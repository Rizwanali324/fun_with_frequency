Part 1: Hybrid Images:

In this project, I have attempted to create Hybrid Images using Computational Photography. Hybrid Images are those images, the perception of whom depend on the viewing distance. They are a combination of two images, such that one is perceived when viewing the hybrid image from up close and the other is perceived when viewing the image from far away. This is done using the low frequency component of the image that is to be perceived from far and on the other hand using the high frequencies of the image that is to be perceived from up close.

Part 2: Gaussian and Laplacian Stacks:

In this part, I delved into the fascinating world of multi-scale image analysis. My goal was to understand the inner workings of an image by dissecting it using Gaussian and Laplacian pyramids/stacks. Imagine these like zooming into a painting and focusing on individual brushstrokes or stepping back to appreciate the overall composition – that's essentially what I did, but at the level of frequencies within the image. By constructing pyramids, I built a condensed version of the image, where each level showcased a different range of frequencies. Like a topographical map highlighting mountains and valleys, these pyramids allowed me to analyze both fine textures and broader structural elements. Stacks, on the other hand, offered me a different perspective, exploring the image at various levels of blur while retaining its original size. This multi-dimensional analysis opens up a treasure trove of possibilities. I can now scrutinize the intricate textures of a fabric, dissect the subtle contrasts in a landscape, or identify crucial objects across different scales. This information is invaluable for tasks like enhancing an image by selectively sharpening details or smoothing noise, focusing on specific frequency bands. While this project didn't delve into image blending directly, the potential is tantalizing. These pyramids/stacks can be used as building blocks to seamlessly merge two images in the frequency domain, creating visually stunning results. This exploration into multi-scale analysis has equipped me with a powerful tool for understanding and manipulating images at different levels. I'm eager to see how this knowledge can be applied in diverse fields, from unraveling the secrets hidden within medical scans to crafting breathtaking visual effects. The possibilities are endless, and I'm excited to continue my journey into the multiverse of images!

Part 3: Multiresolution Blending:

I explored the task of blending two images using mask(s) to create a new, combined output. I successfully loaded and resized two images to the same size for processing. To enable frequency-domain blending, I generated Gaussian and Laplacian pyramids for each image. Then, I combined the Laplacian pyramids element-wise to create a blended pyramid. This blended pyramid allowed me to reconstruct the final blended image. For further customization, I implemented two masking approaches: Simple Mask: I used a left-half mask to selectively reveal portions of each image, creating a distinct blend. Irregular Mask: I utilized a more complex mask with white, black, and gray areas to define specific blending regions, offering more creative control. I displayed and saved the processed images and final blends for evaluation. This allowed me to analyze the results and assess the effectiveness of the approaches. This project demonstrated the effectiveness of image blending with masks and its potential for various applications. By further exploring techniques and customization, this approach can be even more valuable in real-world scenarios.

3 - Pile

In this task, I construct and save Gaussian and Laplacian pyramids, alongside Gaussian and Laplacian stacks, for a given image, leveraging the OpenCV library. My efforts aimed at exploring multi-scale image analysis techniques:

1. **Gaussian Pyramid**:
I created a series of images, each of progressively lower resolution than the original, achieved through Gaussian blurring and subsequent downsampling.
2. **Laplacian Pyramid**:
Building upon the Gaussian Pyramid, I enhanced the image details and edges by subtracting an upsampled lower-resolution image from its higher-resolution counterpart.
3. **Gaussian Stack**:
I maintained the original image size across all levels but increased the blurring effect, differing from the Gaussian Pyramid by avoiding downsampling.
4. **Laplacian Stack**:
to the Laplacian Pyramid but constructed using the Gaussian Stack to highlight the detail differences between successive blur levels, all the while keeping the original image size constant.

4 - Melange Image

My main task was to blend images of an apple and an orange to create a unique "orple" blended image, utilizing advanced image processing techniques with Python and OpenCV. This endeavor was part of a broader exploration into custom blending, masking, and enhancing images. Here's an overview of my work, including the primary goal and the additional techniques I employed:

1. **Creating the "Orple" Blended Image**:

The centerpiece of my project was ingeniously blending the apple and orange images to produce a new, "orple" image. My goal was to merge the distinct characteristics of both fruits into one composite image that smoothly transitions between the features of an apple and an orange.

2. **Custom Blending Techniques**:

Beyond simply creating the "orple" image, I delved into custom blending methods. This involved using both simple geometric masks for straightforward blending and irregular masks for more artistic and nuanced effects. The irregular mask, in particular, allowed me to creatively control the blend, achieving a seamless integration of the two fruits' unique aspects according to the mask's contours.

3. **Mask Application**:

I detailed the process of applying simple geometric masks, which divided the images for a basic blend, and irregular masks, which facilitated the custom blending effects. The geometric approach was direct and simple, whereas the irregular mask offered a sophisticated technique to manipulate the blend area for intricate shapes and transitions.

4. **Enhancement and Visualization**:

I focused on enhancing the "orple" image to highlight its details and adjust its visibility. I utilized Laplacian filters to emphasize edges and textures and applied normalization and histogram equalization to improve the visualization, ensuring that the blended image's details were vivid and clear.
